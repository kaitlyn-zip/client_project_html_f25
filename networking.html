Networking & Building Connections

Why Networking Matters

Networking is one of the most powerful tools in your career development journey. Studies show that more than 70% of jobs are found through networking rather than through cold applications. Employers value connections and referrals because they often highlight candidates who are already trusted by someone within their network.

Beyond landing a job, networking opens doors to valuable opportunities. Through conversations with peers, alumni, and industry professionals, you can gain insider knowledge, discover trends, and uncover roles you may not have even known existed. Strategies for Building Connections

  • Start With Your Circle
    Classmates, professors, project teammates, and alumni are often your strongest connections. Reach out after group work, lectures, or events to continue the conversation.

  • Leverage Online Platforms
    Keep Your LinkedIn profile updated and use it to connect with professionals in your field. Join relevant groups and participate in discussions to increase your visibility. Follow and connect with UMSI Alumni working in your filed of interest. Share or comment on industry articles to stay visible.

  • Attend Events
    Attend UMSI Career Fairs, Career Development Office employer panels, and workships. There are also a plentiful amount of student organizations that host networking events. Look for events hosted by professional associations in your field, both locally and nationally. Conferences, seminars, and meetups are great places to meet like-minded professionals.
